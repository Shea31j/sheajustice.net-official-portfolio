About Me

The story so far

I'm based in Farmington, Maine — a small town that's big on community and fresh air. My career has taken me across a few different worlds: production work at a wood pellet mill, patient care in a nursing home, retail at Hannaford, and for the past three years, state government work as part of the scan team at Maine's Department of Health and Human Services. That range of experience taught me how to show up, adapt, and work alongside all kinds of people — and it made me curious about the software running behind all of it.

What I love most about programming is that it rewards curiosity and persistence above everything else. Python was my first language and it's still my strongest — there's something satisfying about how readable and expressive it is.

I'm part of a growing wave of Black developers reshaping what the tech industry looks like. When I'm not coding, you'll find me on a hiking trail, at the bowling alley, deep in a grand strategy game, or working through my reading list.
